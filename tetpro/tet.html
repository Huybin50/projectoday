About Tet:


Date:

Tet falls on the first night of the new moon in the first month of the Lunar Calendar, which in the Gregorian calendar usually takes place between the last week of January and the third week of February.


History:

The biggest, oldest, and most widely celebrated traditional celebration in Vietnam is Tet Nguyen Dan, generally referred to as Lunar New Year. Our forebears have observed the yearly Tet holiday year since the prehistoric Ly, Tran, and Le kingdoms.


How does Tet holiday means to Vietnam?

Tet falls on the first night of the new moon in the first month of the Lunar Calendar, which in the Gregorian calendar usually takes place between the last week of January and the third week of February.
